P01 · Q2 Confirmed
Website GEO Optimisation
Generative Engine Optimisation · AI search visibility for Zero Markets
Why we do this
AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google SGE) are reshaping how users discover financial products. Traditional SEO rankings have almost no influence on AI-generated answers. Zero Markets currently has near-zero citation presence in AI responses, while competitors are beginning to invest. This is an open window — AI recommendation positions are not yet consolidated, and brands that establish authority now will hold a structural advantage.
Reference documents
Q2-GEO-Plan (SharePoint)
OKR
Objective
Establish Zero Markets as a citable, AI-visible authority in key trading and forex queries
Complete full-site GEO audit; identify and document 10+ AI visibility gaps
Deploy Schema.org markup (FAQPage, Organization, BreadcrumbList) across 5+ core pages
Achieve brand citation in ChatGPT / Perplexity for 3 target query types
Establish AI visibility monitoring baseline; tracked monthly
GEO vs SEO
GEO targets AI citation, not keyword rankings. The logic: structured content + authority signals + direct question answering. A page that ranks #1 on Google may still never appear in an AI-generated response if it lacks the right structure.
How to execute

Content audit (April) — Review core site pages (homepage, product, regulatory, FAQ); identify content structures most likely to be cited by AI
2
Schema deployment (April–May) — Add FAQPage, Organization, and BreadcrumbList structured data markup across priority pages
3
FAQ / help content rewrite (May) — Restructure in direct Q&A format targeting real user queries in AI tools
4
Authority signal enhancement (May) — Add regulatory credentials page, authoritative external references, media endorsement module
5
AI visibility monitoring (June) — Regular testing in ChatGPT / Perplexity for brand citation; establish monthly tracking baseline

P03 · Q2 Confirmed
Access Management System
Centralised SaaS permission management across 17 systems · 4 structured workflows
Why we're building this — 5 core problems
Problem 01
No formal access request process
Access granted via chat messages with no approval chain, no records, and no audit trail.
Problem 02
No unified entry point for personnel changes
Title changes, team transfers, and promotions handled separately — same person has inconsistent identities across systems.
Problem 03
SaaS costs out of control
Adobe CC and Similarweb per-seat licences continue billing after employees leave or change roles.
Problem 04
Team Leaders have no visibility
Managers don't know which systems their team members have access to — can't verify appropriateness or take audit responsibility.
Problem 05
Offboarding relies on memory across 17 systems
When employees leave, IT and HR mentally recall which systems to revoke — across Jira, Monday.com, Meta Ads, Instagram, Confluence, CRM, Funding Admin, IB Portal, Marketing Portal, Risk Portal, Admin Portal, Superset, Adobe CC, Similarweb, HubSpot, Message Template, and Meta Ads Manager. Entirely unsustainable.
System links
Access Management System (Live) Process Flow Design (Live)
4 workflows designed
1
Access Request — Employee submits → Team Leader approves in-system → IT executes → full audit log auto-generated. Solves Problem 01.
2
Offboarding — HR triggers → system auto-generates full permission checklist → IT revokes per item → completion certificate issued. 8 internal systems via API, 9 external manually confirmed. Solves Problems 03 + 05.
3
Personnel Change — Manager submits → HR as single source of truth → IT syncs all 17 systems → role-linked permissions auto-triggered. Solves Problem 02.
4
Quarterly Access Review — IT initiates each quarter → Team Leaders review and flag anomalies → IT processes flags → audit report generated. Solves Problem 04.
How to execute in Q2
1
Stakeholder alignment (April) — Confirm rollout scope with IT, HR, and department managers; requires Paddy endorsement to drive cross-team cooperation
2
Data initialisation — Audit all 70+ employees' current SaaS permissions across 17 systems; input into system to establish access baseline
3
System testing & deployment (May) — End-to-end testing of all 4 workflows: request flow, approval flow, offboarding flow, quarterly review
4
Onboarding / offboarding SOP — Standardised permission workflows integrated with HR processes; documented and signed off
5
Full rollout & training (June) — Train department managers and Team Leaders on system usage; assign module owners per workflow
